Atlas

Lead Designer • Tideworks • 2020

Port terminal logistics is a world of precision; where every container, crane, and corridor needs to be accounted for before a single plan can be made. Yet the tools available to terminal planners were never built with them in mind. The existing workflow relied on a complex 3D mapping tool that demanded anywhere between 200 to 400 hours per terminal, a process as tedious as it was time-consuming, leaving little room for agility or scale.

Atlas was designed to change that. Sitting at the crossroads of the visual familiarity of Photoshop and the spatial intelligence of Google Maps, Atlas gave terminal planners a purpose-built environment to map port terminals with precision using terminal-specific objects to generate coordinates directly usable by planning systems for container tracking and logistics.

The result was transformative. By grounding the design in real user stories and business requirements, Atlas reduced terminal mapping time by over 75%, turning a weeks-long process into one that could be completed in a fraction of the time, without sacrificing accuracy or detail.

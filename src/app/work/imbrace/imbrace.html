Imbrace

Individual Contributor • Thesis - Parsons School of Design • 2017

Low back pain is one of the most common conditions women live with, and yet the solutions available rarely speak to them as individuals. Most options come from the medical world, designed for function over feeling, while commercial alternatives focus on prevention rather than providing real, immediate relief. Through all of this, women are left choosing between managing their pain and maintaining their sense of self.

Why should a health solution make you feel clinical, different, or less like yourself? Fashion and personal expression are deeply tied to identity, the way they present themselves to the world matters. Yet when it comes to back pain, that identity is often the first thing asked to be set aside.

Imbrace was born from this gap. It sits at the crossroads of fashion and medicine — a wearable that offers immediate ease while blending seamlessly into everyday style. It doesn't ask women to compromise. Instead, it invites them to embrace their pain as part of their story, and do something about it on their own terms.
